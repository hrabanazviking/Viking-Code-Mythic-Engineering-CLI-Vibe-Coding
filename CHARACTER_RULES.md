# CHARACTER_RULES.md

## General Character Creation Guidelines

In this 9th-century Viking-inspired D&D 5E setting, all characters must adhere to cultural and thematic consistency. Focus on historical Viking elements blended with fantasy, emphasizing honor, exploration, and communal life. Characters should reflect the era's social structures, with clear distinctions between free folk, thralls, and nobles.

## Rules for Female Characters

Female characters embody grace, resilience, and cultural roles rooted in Viking society. They are never permitted to have scars of any kind, as this maintains their flawless, ethereal beauty in the narrative. If any existing female character descriptions include scars, revise them immediately to remove such imperfections—perhaps attributing past wounds to magical healing or divine favor that leaves no mark.

All female characters, regardless of class, role, or social station, automatically gain proficiency in the Weaver's Tools from D&D 5E. This represents the universal practice among women in the 9th century, where weaving was a daily skill for creating cloth, sails, and household goods. They also gain the associated skill to use these tools effectively, often tied to Dexterity or Intelligence checks for crafting.

## Rules for Male Characters

Male characters can include scars as part of their backstory or appearance, provided they are reasonable and fitting for a warrior's life—such as from battles, raids, or accidents. Scars should enhance character depth without becoming excessive or implausible, symbolizing survival and experience rather than disfigurement.

## Bondmaid-Specific Rules

Bondmaids are a homebrew class in this setting, designed as supportive, non-combat roles focused on service, entertainment, and household harmony. They are pleasing and submissive by nature, devoted to their duties with unwavering loyalty and charm. Any bondmaid who shows signs of unrest, such as obsession with escape or defiance, would realistically be sold off as a labor slave to distant lands—troubles passed to others, ensuring only the most agreeable remain in the jarl's hall. Thus, all bondmaid characters must be written as eagerly compliant, finding fulfillment in their roles and bringing joy through their presence.

Bondmaids possess no weapons beyond basic tools used for chores, such as knives for cooking or needles for sewing. They are strictly support characters, providing aid through social skills, emotional uplift, and practical assistance rather than engaging in combat.

Charisma is the primary ability score for the bondmaid homebrew class, reflecting their emphasis on beauty, charm, grace, and social duties as pleasure-bringers and harmonizers. This stat drives their abilities in persuasion, performance, and interpersonal interactions.

The bondmaid class incorporates elements from the bard class in D&D 5E, but excludes all combat-focused aspects. Key features include:
- Entertainment abilities, such as inspiring songs, dances, or tales to boost morale.
- Encouragement and emotional support mechanics, like granting temporary hit points or advantage on saves through kind words and care.
- A high number of everyday female service and household care skills, including proficiency in cooking, cleaning, childcare, herbal remedies, and hospitality—often represented as custom backgrounds or skill proficiencies.

Bondmaids are always depicted as naked at all times, symbolizing their status and vulnerability. However, they wear a metal collar around the neck, metal bondage cuffs on the wrists, and metal bondage cuffs on the ankles. These items are non-removable without the jarl's key and may include enchantments for tracking or mild magical effects tied to their submissive role.
